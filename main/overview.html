Overview

The French Revolution was a period in which citizens in France ousted the monarchy and took control of the country. The French Revolution began on July 14, 1789, and lasted for ten years, from 1789 to 1799. It was a time of great social upheaval and chaos. The French Revolution was a bloody revolt that toppled France's monarchy. Its goal was to alter the interaction between rulers and citizens. The revolution has begun. There were various fundamental elements that contributed to citizens' dissatisfaction with the monarchy; there were also numerous substantial consequences for France and other countries following the revolution.
